# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard | Employee Attrition & Compensation Analysis

An interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built using **Microsoft Excel** to analyze employee attrition, compensation, and workforce trends. The project leverages Pivot Tables, GETPIVOTDATA, KPI Cards, Slicers, and dynamic visualizations to help HR professionals identify workforce challenges and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project consists of two interactive dashboards:

### 1. Employee Attrition Analytics Dashboard
Analyzes employee turnover across multiple HR dimensions to identify the key factors influencing attrition.

### 2. Employee Compensation Analytics Dashboard
Provides insights into payroll, salary distribution, salary hikes, and compensation trends across departments and job roles.

---

# 📊 Dashboard 1 – Employee Attrition Analytics

## KPIs

- Total Employees
- Total Attrition
- Attrition Rate

## Analysis Performed

- Attrition Rate by Department
- Attrition Rate by Job Role
- Attrition Rate by Age Group
- Attrition Rate by Gender
- Attrition Rate by Education
- Attrition Rate by Overtime
- Attrition Rate by Years in Current Role

---

### Key Insights

- Sales Representatives have the highest attrition rate (39.8%).
- Employees working overtime experience nearly three times higher attrition than employees who do not work overtime.
- Employees aged 18–27 show the highest turnover.
- Sales department records the highest attrition among all departments.
- Attrition decreases as employees spend more years in their current role.

---

# 💰 Dashboard 2 – Employee Compensation Analytics

## KPIs

- Total Payroll
- Average Monthly Salary
- Average Salary Hike

## Analysis Performed

- Total Payroll by Department
- Average Salary by Department
- Average Salary by Job Role
- Average Salary by Job Level
- Employee Distribution by Salary Hike
- Average Salary Hike by Performance Rating

---

### Key Insights

- Research & Development has the highest payroll expenditure.
- Managers receive the highest average monthly salary.
- Salary increases consistently with job level.
- Employees with higher performance ratings receive significantly larger salary hikes.
- More than 55% of employees receive salary hikes between 11% and 14%, indicating a standardized annual increment policy.
